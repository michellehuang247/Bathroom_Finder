Our Mission

It's important to include people of all communities. Learn some more about the communites our website reaches out to.

Transgender

Transgender people have a gender identity or gender expression different from their assigned sex (which is identified at birth). The term "transgender" is often referred to as an umbrella term, as it encompasses people who are non-binary, meaning that they are not expressively feminine or masculine, as well as people who are genderqueer or genderfluid, meaning that they identify with neither, both, or a combination of male and female genders.

Members of the transgender community often have trouble finding a restroom that makes them feel welcome. We hope our website will help more people find more safe bathrooms for their needs.

Physically Disabled

People who are physically disabled generally have a limitation on their physical functioning, mobility, dexterity (flexibility), or stamina. Prenatal disabilities are acquired before birth, while post-natal disabilities are acquired after birth. Perinatal disabilities are acquired any time between a few weeks before to a few weeks after birth.
Mobility, visual, and hearing impairments are all types of physical disabilities.

With our website, members of the physically disabled community can easily locate a nearby bathroom that conforms to their needs. These bathrooms have grab rail supports and various other features designed to make things easier.

Parents of Babies

"Behind every young child who believes in himself is a parent who believed first"
-anonymous

There's no doubt that parents are a vital part of our community. Parents are responsible for raising each new generation that comes into our world!

Unfortunately, many parents have trouble finding a nearby safe and sanitary place to change their children's diapers. The goal of our website is to make the lives parents all over a bit easier, by providing a service that quickly finds a nearby restrooms with changing tables.
